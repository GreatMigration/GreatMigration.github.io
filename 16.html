Wages Today




Today Black Americans still feel the burden that their forefathers did, with wages that are still significantly less than their white counterparts. The Economic Policy Institute found that black men get paid only 71% of what white men do, and that this can’t be solely due to educational and job differences. “At the end of the day, it turns out that being black matters,” even half a century after Jim Crow was overturned.

The Economic Policy Institute also addressed common misconceptions that have come about to try to demonstrate that we live in a post-racial society. One of these is the idea that native-born blacks do less-well than their non-native counterparts. But Patrick Mason, a professor of Economics at Florida State University, notes that black immigrants make still less than their native counter-parts.
