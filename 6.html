George Starling




George Sterling faced a unique situation compared to Ida Mae and Dr. Foster. While the other two rarely returned to the South, and even then only for funerals, George found himself in a position where he returned to the South many times a week while working train routes up and down the eastern seaboard. For years George looked Jim Crow in the eye every time that he crossed out of Washington DC and into Virginia, and then the liberation of the return trip. George was able to see the difference between the North and South, and experienced the same discrimination Dr. Foster had.
