Suburban Deception




Freeport Village in New York was the epitome of racially charged housing discrimination during the Great Migration. Home to the worst living conditions in the state, the New York State Housing Commission offered a 741 thousand dollar loan in order to finance the building of 100 new apartments to replace the dilapidated housing. Instead of taking the money however it was put to a vote that only property owners, who had a fiscal incentive to say no, and the vote failed. This meant that there would be no new housing for the predominately black people of Freeport, and they would continue to pay high prices for terrible living situations.
