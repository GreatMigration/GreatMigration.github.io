Deeper than Law




When the characters in The Warmth of Other Suns escaped the South they faced racism in ways that they hadn’t anticipated. They found that law only went so far in protecting them from the same dangers they faced in the South. Southern Blacks were subject to constant fear because they knew that they weren’t equal on a legal level. Beyond legal standing though there was also the constant fear of any crimes committed against them going unpunished.

By escaping the South blacks were only escaping part of the racism that they had faced. As described in a few personal anecdotes of those featured in The Warmth of Other Suns, there is a still a clear racial barrier that needs to be overcome, and as time passes it becomes increasingly apparent that racism by fact is much harder to change than racism by law.
