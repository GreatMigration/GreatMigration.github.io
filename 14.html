Wages




Economically it would seem that black southerners would be better off in the North where wages were higher. Many southern blacks worked for measly wages mostly working the fields in some way or another. Even those who had jobs that should have made for a good living like teachers found that they got paid far less, even half of what their white counterparts made. The North may have offered better pay during the war, but other factors diminished the value that the North had financially.
